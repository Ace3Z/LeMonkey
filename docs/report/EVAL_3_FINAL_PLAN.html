LeMonkey Eval 3 — Pi0.5 Strategy

Post-SmolVLA pivot · 2-day remaining sprint · 2026-05-20 cross-validated by 4 research agents
⚖ Strategic pivot — 2026-05-20

The team has empirically confirmed via attention-map analysis that SmolVLA's VLM head puts essentially zero attention weight on the correct celebrity face when prompted with names. Every SmolVLA recipe we tried (vanilla, Hans's warm-VLM, Mahbod's M2 ArcFace toolkit) failed to bind faces to names. SmolVLA is structurally inadequate for fine-grained named-entity recognition at our scale.

The whole team has converged on Pi0.5-3B as the only viable backbone — primarily because of its PaliGemma vision-language head, which is trained on a substantially larger and more entity-aware corpus than SmolVLM2's pretraining mix.

This document is the new 2-day plan: 3 parallel Pi0.5 tracks, each testing a different mechanism for adding face-discrimination capability to PaliGemma.

⚠ Critical correction from 2026-05-20 research

Pi0.5 uses PaliGemma 1, not PaliGemma 2. Verified from lerobot/pi05_base/config.json: paligemma_variant=gemma_2b (Gemma 1 2B). PaliGemma 2's entity-tuned WebLI splits (Entity-WebLI, OVEN benchmark) do not apply to our backbone. PG1 has a structural ceiling for fine-grained recognition — confirmed by our 2026-05-09 0/14 zero-shot probe. This means the VLM must be actively taught face-id capability during fine-tuning; we cannot rely on the pretrained prior alone.

§1 · Why we pivoted: the SmolVLA failure mode

The failure is reproducible and now well-characterised. When SmolVLA is given the prompt "Place the coke on Taylor Swift" and the wrist cam shows three printed portraits, the VLM head's cross-attention map between text tokens and image patches puts no extra weight on the correct face. The action expert defaults to a positional shortcut from the training distribution — placing the can wherever the named celeb most often appeared during teleop.

The literature has two compatible explanations:

  • (G1) Pretrain distribution. SmolVLM2 was pretrained on FineWeb-Edu subsets + PixelProse — neither has documented entity-tuned splits. Long-tail named entities are systematically underrepresented (Parashar 2024, arxiv 2401.12425).
  • (G2) Architectural bottleneck. SmolVLM2 compresses each image to 64 tokens via 2×2 pixel-shuffle. For a workspace with three small printed portraits, that's ~4-6 tokens per face — well below the threshold where SigLIP's face-discriminative geometry survives.

The OpenVLA-OFT+ paper (arxiv 2502.19645) provides an independent confirmation: removing their FiLM modulation on the vision tower drops multi-object discrimination to chance level. Our SmolVLA's standard cross-attention is the same architectural class as OFT-without-FiLM.

Why Pi0.5 (PaliGemma 1) gives us a fighting chance

  • PaliGemma 1's vision tower is SigLIP-So400m (the same as SmolVLA), but it produces 256+ tokens per image (not 64) — much more capacity for face-region discrimination.
  • PaliGemma's LM is Gemma 1 2B (4.4× more parameters than SmolVLM2's 500M LM). Larger LMs are known to retain rarer named entities (REAL, arxiv 2401.12425; Pi0.5 §3 ablations).
  • The Pi0.5-KI paper (arxiv 2505.23705 §4) reports that even with no architectural changes, Pi0.5's PaliGemma backbone — when properly trained — supports fine-grained semantic conditioning that frozen-VLM Pi0.5 lacks.

But: PaliGemma 1 was not trained on Entity-WebLI splits. That tuning only exists in PaliGemma 2 (arxiv 2412.03555). Pi0.5 is locked to PG1@224. So we cannot rely on the pretrained prior alone for celeb-level discrimination — we have to actively teach the VLM the 200 celeb identities during fine-tuning. This is the load-bearing decision behind every track below.

§2 · The 4 ideas on the table — research verdict

The team identified 4 candidate approaches. After cross-validation by 4 parallel research agents (see §7), here's how each holds up against the published literature.

Idea Description Paper backing Verdict
1. Vanilla LoRA on 200-celeb dataset Pi0.5 LoRA fine-tune on HBOrtiz/so101_eval3_aug_v3_200celebs with no extra mechanism. Weak. Pi0.5-KI (2505.23705) explicitly says "fully freezing VLM yields ~0%"; they update the full backbone with co-training. LoRA was not tested in any published Pi0.5 ablation. LangGap (2603.00592) warns fine-grained discrimination needs thousands of task-specific examples — we have 50/celeb. Run as baseline only. Don't expect it to win.
2. PaliGemma VQA pretrain → VLA finetune Two-stage: warm PaliGemma on VGGFace2 VQA, then VLA-finetune on the robot dataset. Mixed. Pi0.5 itself uses two-stage (FAST-only pretrain → flow-matching post-train), so the pattern is precedented. But post-Pi0.5-KI (2505.23705) the field moved to co-training. 3. ArcFace ↔ SigLIP latent alignment Train an MLP projector + cosine alignment loss between PaliGemma's SigLIP patches and a frozen ArcFace teacher's 512-dim embedding. Genuine research-grade contribution. Worth running. Real defensible novel result.
4. Pi0.5 fine-tune on 10k 200-celeb dataset Same as Option 1. Listed separately by the team but functionally identical. Same as Option 1. Merge with Option 1.
★ Recipe NOT on the team's original list, but strongest paper backing

ObjectVLA-style co-training (arxiv 2502.19250) directly tackles "robot sees novel object but can't bind name → action" — verbatim our failure mode. The recipe:

  • Mix robot demonstrations with vision-language pairs at 10:1 ratio in a single training run (not sequential).
  • Each VL pair includes explicit bounding-box grounding: "the face of Yann LeCun is at [x1, y1, x2, y2]".
  • Without the bboxes, their OOD success drops 64% → 19%. Grounding is the load-bearing piece.

We can generate ~15k bbox-grounded VL pairs from our 193 scraped celeb photos by running RetinaFace on each (already installed on edna). This becomes our Track 2 below.

Track 1 — Vanilla LoRA (baseline)

owner: Roham backbone: lerobot/pi05_base output: HBOrtiz/pi05_eval3_track_B (3-celeb, in flight) + future 200-celeb push

Why we're keeping the in-flight run. Even though LoRA isn't validated by any published Pi0.5 ablation, the run is 58% complete and gives us a clean baseline for comparison. Killing it now wastes 17k steps. After Day-3 Strix data we'll know if it works on 3 celebs; if it does, we then know whether the 200-celeb expansion (Tracks 1b / 2 / 3) adds value.

Track 2 — ObjectVLA co-train ★ (highest-evidence)

owner: Sejohn (training) + Darius (VL-data + Strix) backbone: lerobot/pi05_base output: HBOrtiz/pi05_eval3_objectvla where: brev_instance2 (RTX PRO 6000 96GB) runbook: TRACK_OBJECTVLA.md

Why this is the strongest single recipe. ObjectVLA (arxiv 2502.19250) is the only published method that directly tackles our failure mode (named-entity → action binding) and reports quantitative gains. 10:1 robot:VL co-training with bbox-grounded captions: without the bboxes, OOD success drops 64% → 19%. This is the load-bearing intervention, validated end-to-end.

Independent confirmation comes from Pi0.5's own ablation (arxiv 2504.16054 Fig. 11): removing web co-training drops OOD object success from ~75% to ~45-50% (a ~25-30pp gap).

Data preparation (~2-4h on edna)

  1. Generate bbox-grounded VL pairs from the 193-celeb scraped bank. Per photo:
    • Run InsightFace RetinaFace detector (already installed at ~/miniconda3/envs/aug on edna).
    • Extract face bbox [x1, y1, x2, y2] normalized to image dims.
    • Emit 2-3 caption variants per photo:
      • "The face of Yann LeCun is at [0.21, 0.32, 0.58, 0.74]." (location-explicit, ObjectVLA-style)
      • "Who is the person at [0.21, 0.32, 0.58, 0.74]?" → "Yann LeCun" (Q&A form)
      • "Yann LeCun is in this image." (caption form, no bbox — small fraction for variety)
    Deliverable: ~15k VL pairs as parquet manifest. ~1.5k photos × 10 captions/photo.
  2. Optionally enrich with our 200-celeb dataset's wrist-cam frames (not yet implemented). For each augmented teleop episode, sample 5 frames + emit similar VL captions. This adds the actual print/lighting distribution. ~50k more pairs.
    Deliverable: extended manifest at HBOrtiz/eval3_objectvla_vl_pairs

Training (~24h on brev_instance2)

This requires a small patch to lerobot-train to support mixed batches: 10 robot batches per 1 VL batch in the same optimizer step. The VL batches go through Pi0.5's PaliGemmaWithExpertModel.forward() in VQA mode (no action expert), the robot batches use the standard VLA forward. Loss is summed.

# Pseudocode for the mixed-batch trainer
for step in range(num_steps):
    if step % 11 == 0:  # 10:1 ratio per ObjectVLA
        batch = vl_dataloader.next()
        loss = compute_vqa_loss(batch)  # PaliGemma + lm_head CE
    else:
        batch = robot_dataloader.next()
        loss = compute_pi05_flow_loss(batch)  # standard Pi0.5 path
    loss.backward(); optimizer.step()

Implementation note: We have train_paligemma_vqa.py already scaffolded from the warm-start plan; it provides the VQA-mode forward. The patch to mix it into lerobot-train is ~150 lines.

Why this beats the two-stage VQA-warmstart we originally planned

  • No catastrophic forgetting risk. The action expert sees PaliGemma's actual training trajectory, not a backbone shifted by a separate VQA stage.
  • Direct paper validation. 64% → 19% OOD-drop number is a real ablation, not an extrapolation.
  • Better data signal. The bbox-grounded captions teach SigLIP where in the image the face is, not just that it's a face — this is exactly the supervision the attention map analysis says is missing.

Track 3 — ArcFace ↔ SigLIP alignment (novel research contribution)

owner: Mahbod backbone: lerobot/pi05_base + frozen ArcFace buffalo_l/w600k_r50 output: HBOrtiz/pi05_eval3_arcface_aligned runbook: TRACK_ARCFACE.md

Mahbod already built the M2 ArcFace cosine-distillation toolkit for SmolVLA — he carries the ArcFace expertise into this Pi0.5 port.

Why this is worth running even though it's novel. BlindVLA (arxiv 2510.25616) validated the mechanism — frozen MLP projector + per-token cosine alignment loss at λ=0.2 between LM hidden states and a frozen teacher's features. Their teacher was a general ViT (C-RADIOv3); no published paper has tried this with ArcFace specifically as the teacher. If it works, it's a real research-grade contribution; if it doesn't, the failure is informative.

The orthogonal hypothesis from Track 2: Track 2 says SigLIP doesn't know where the face is. Track 3 says SigLIP doesn't have face-discriminative geometry. Both could be true and both could be needed; running them independently lets us decompose.

Architecture

$$L_{total} = L_{flow\_matching} + \lambda \cdot L_{align}$$ $$L_{align} = -\frac{1}{k}\sum_{j=1}^{k} \cos\left(F.normalize(\text{MLP}(z^{arcface}_j)),\ F.normalize(h^{pg}_j)\right), \quad \lambda = 0.2$$

where z^arcface is the 512-dim ArcFace embedding of the face crop at frame j, MLP is a frozen 2-layer MLP (512 → 2048 → 2048 with GELU + LayerNorm) trained for 1k warmup steps on the 192-celeb bank, and h^pg is the LM hidden state at the face-token position in PaliGemma's middle layer (~layer 9 of Gemma 2B).

Subtasks

  1. Build the projector pretrain dataset. Pairs of (face crop, identity label) from the 192-celeb bank. ~1.5k pairs.
    Deliverable: HBOrtiz/eval3_arcface_projector_pretrain (small)
  2. Pretrain the MLP projector (~1h on any GPU). ArcFace → MLP → match to PaliGemma's mean-pooled face-token features from a vanilla forward pass. Save as a frozen module.
    Deliverable: HBOrtiz/eval3_arcface_projector
  3. Patch modeling_pi05.py to: (a) at training time, extract face bbox via InsightFace, (b) compute ArcFace embedding, (c) project through frozen MLP, (d) compute auxiliary cosine loss against LM hidden state at face-token position in layer 9.
  4. Train Pi0.5 + frozen projector + cosine aux loss on so101_eval3_aug_v3_200celebs. Same LoRA recipe as Track 1 plus the auxiliary head. ~24h.
    Deliverable: HBOrtiz/pi05_eval3_arcface_aligned

Risks

  • Inference-time face detection adds latency. ArcFace + RetinaFace runs ~30-50 ms on Strix. We have 20 s/rollout budget; trivial.
  • The face bbox is needed at inference too. We can either (a) run RetinaFace at inference (still within budget), or (b) train without bbox and align at every patch (lossier but simpler).
  • If smile/expression varies between celeb's photos and the printed-photo eval condition, ArcFace embeddings may not match. We'd have to augment with print-style transforms during projector pretrain. ~few hours of dev effort.

§7 · Research evidence base (4 parallel agents, 2026-05-20)

Per CLAUDE.md §9 (cross-validate load-bearing claims with parallel agents) and §7 (triple-source numerical defaults), we ran 4 independent research agents on 2026-05-20 to validate this strategy against published literature. Their summaries:

Agent 1 — VLA + fine-grained named-entity recognition SOTA

  • ObjectVLA (2502.19250) is the closest paper to our problem. 10:1 robot:VL co-train; without bbox-grounding OOD drops 64% → 19%. Verified via WebFetch on arxiv HTML.
  • Pi0.5-KI (2505.23705) does not validate LoRA. Their recipe is full backbone update + action-expert stop-gradient + FAST CE on lm_head. Our Track 1 LoRA recipe is paper-extrapolation, not paper-validated.
  • OpenVLA-OFT+ (2502.19645): FiLM is necessary for language-grounded discrimination. Removing FiLM drops multi-object success to chance. Independent confirmation of our SmolVLA attention-map diagnosis.
  • Pi0.6 / RECAP (2511.14759) is irrelevant. Offline RL for execution recovery, not language grounding.

Agent 2 — ArcFace ↔ VLM alignment

  • BlindVLA (2510.25616) is the only published alignment recipe. λ=0.2, frozen MLP projector (4096→2048→768), mid-layer cosine loss. Teacher is C-RADIOv3 (general ViT), NOT ArcFace.
  • No published paper aligns ArcFace specifically with a VLM/VLA. Face-LLaVA, FaceInsight, FaceLLM, Face-MLLM all use landmarks/FLIP/face-VQA — none use ArcFace embedding injection. Our Track 3 would be the first.
  • MLP projector beats cross-attention at our data scale. LLaVA-1.5 ablation; Q-Former needs 10²-10³× more data than we have.
  • Frozen teacher is unanimous in the published literature (BlindVLA, Arc2Face, FR distillation lit).

Agent 3 — VQA-pretrain → VLA fine-tune precedents

  • Pi0.5 itself is two-stage (FAST-only pretrain α=0 → flow-matching post-train α=10). But the field has moved to co-training.
  • VLM2VLA (2509.22195) explicitly argues sequential VQA-pretrain causes forgetting. LoRA-only on the backbone with co-training preserves 85% of base VLM capability across 12 benchmarks.
  • LangGap (2603.00592) supports VQA pretrain for fine-grained discrimination, but warns thousands of task-specific examples are needed.
  • Pi0.5-KI Fig. 11: removing web co-train data drops OOD from ~75% to ~45-50%. ~25-30 pp gap. Strong quantitative argument for any-kind-of-VL signal during training.
  • Verdict: co-training > two-stage. Hence the team's Option 2 (VQA warmstart) is suboptimal; Track 2 (ObjectVLA co-train) is preferred.

Agent 4 — Pi0.5 ecosystem + alternatives

  • Pi0.5 uses PaliGemma 1, not PaliGemma 2. Verified via config.json: paligemma_variant=gemma_2b. PG2's Entity-WebLI tuning doesn't apply to us. Backbone has a structural ceiling.
  • Pi0.5-KI not implemented in lerobot (openpi issue #649 + lerobot README confirms). Only flow-matching head supported. We can't run KI without ~3-5 days of port work.
  • Pi0-FAST autoregressive variant: not better for our failure mode. Discrete action gradients don't help face→name binding; the gradient signal is in the wrong channel.
  • pi05_droid variants: negative value for us — DROID is Franka 7-DoF, our SO-101 is 6-DoF. Action prior pollution.
  • Strix bf16 inference fits at batch=1. ~8 GB weights + 2-3 GB activations + 4-5 GB margin on the 16 GB card. No quantization needed.
  • No alt VLA (TinyVLA, X-VLA, FlowerVLA, OpenVLA, RoboFlamingo, RT-2) has demonstrated face-binding — staying on Pi0.5 is the best public bet.
